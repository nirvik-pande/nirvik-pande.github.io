about me

I'm from Austin, Texas, and growing up there gave me a pretty direct view of how policy decisions play out in practice. That interest in how systems are structured and how they affect people is part of what drew me toward computer science and machine learning. I like problems that have real structure underneath them, even when they look messy on the surface.

The same thing is true of why I care about philosophy. I concentrate in it alongside CS at CMU, and the areas I find most interesting are philosophy of religion and formal epistemology — fields that ask hard questions about belief, justification, and reasoning under uncertainty, with more overlap with ML than most people expect.

At CMU I'm doing research at the Biorobotics Lab on heuristic optimization, finding solutions that are good enough when finding the best possible solution is computationally out of reach. Outside of research I work on AI safety, both technically and on the policy side.
